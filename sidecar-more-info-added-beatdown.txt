     Sidecar-Driven AI Decisions in Forge

     Context

     The Forge AI today is fully heuristic (forge-ai package). A sidecar LLM was
      added to recognize the human's archetype and emit "piloting advice", but
     the advice is currently:

     - Static — _local_piloting_advice() in
     forge-llm-sidecar/app/nodes/game_advisor.py produces fixed percentages
     (e.g. PLAY_SPELL=65 if aggro) with no knowledge of the actual board, hand, 
     or graveyard.
     - Untargeted — ActionScore.target for PLAY_SPELL/PLAY_LAND is currently a
     sentence of English prose from the guide phase plan, not a card name. The
     Java-side match-by-name in AiController.trySidecarRecommendedPlay()
     therefore almost never fires.
     - Partial — only PLAY_SPELL / PLAY_LAND / ATTACK / BLOCK / PASS /
     ACTIVATE_ABILITY are modeled. There is nothing on hand valuation, target
     selection, mulligan, discard, or what the human likely holds.
     - State-blind — the RecognitionRequest Pydantic schema already declares
     hand, own_board, opponent_board, your_graveyard, opponent_graveyard,
     life_totals, but the Java caller (DeckRecognitionObserver.fireCall()) only
     fills observations + deckCards. The fields exist on the wire but never get
     populated.

     Goal: make the sidecar's guidance the actual brain behind Forge's AI plays
     — choosing which lands and spells to play and when, valuing cards in hand,
     anticipating what the human holds, picking targets, and assigning the
     beatdown / control role per Mike Flores's classical doctrine ("Who's the
     Beatdown?", StarCityGames) so all of the above is colored by who is the
     aggressor in the current matchup right now. Based on live game state. The
     existing /recognize stays at one LLM call per turn (deterministic on the
     piloting side, per user direction). Everything is fail-soft: if the sidecar

     app/nodes/game_advisor.py — rewrite _local_piloting_advice(state) to be
     board-aware. The function already has state["piloting_guide"] and
     state["candidate_archetypes"]; now also read state["hand"],
     state["own_board"], state["opponent_board"], state["your_graveyard"],
     state["opponent_graveyard"], state["life_totals"]. New deterministic
     helpers (all in game_advisor.py, or split into app/advice/ if it grows past
      ~200 lines):

     - _assess_role(state, ai_strategy, opp_strategy) → RoleAssessment.
     Implements Mike Flores's "Who's the Beatdown?" doctrine deterministically:
       - Natural role from archetype strategy: aggro/burn/tempo → beatdown;
     control/ramp → control; combo/midrange → context-dependent.
       - Board pressure score per side: sum of power on board + haste/evasion
     bonus + clock (turns to lethal at current life given current attackers).
       - Card advantage score per side: hand size + permanents − graveyard rate,
      with a phase scaling factor.
       - Role flip rule (this is the crux of the article): if the natural-aggro
     side is behind on board pressure AND behind on card advantage, it flips to
     control role; if the natural-control side has clear board+card-advantage
     dominance and a finite clock to lethal, it flips to beatdown.
       - winning_side and margin come from a weighted combination of (life
     delta, board pressure delta, card advantage delta, clock asymmetry).
       - This is consulted by the other helpers below so a single role decision
     colors the rest of the advice.
     - _score_hand(hand, guide, phase, available_mana, role) →
     list[HandValuation]. Uses guide.key_cards roles, CMC vs available mana,
     redundancy in hand, and phase. Lands score by color need. Win-cons get a
     phase-dependent boost. Role modulation: when AI is beatdown, threats and
     burn outrank answers; when AI is control, answers and card draw outrank
     threats; when role-flipped, the modulation flips.
     - _card_specific_actions(hand, guide, phase, opponent_board, life_totals, 
     role) → list of ActionScore with concrete card names in target. Picks the
     single highest-valued playable card per category given current mana and
     board. Role modulation: beatdown mode raises PLAY_SPELL/ATTACK percentages
     and lowers PASS; control mode raises PASS and ACTIVATE_ABILITY (hold-up);
     role flips override the deck's natural defaults. Multiple PLAY_SPELL
     entries allowed so the Java side can fall through if its first pick is
     unplayable.
     - _infer_opponent_hand(observations, opponent_archetype, 
     candidate_archetypes, turn, opponent_graveyard, opponent_board) →
     list[OpponentHandGuess]. For the inferred archetype, take its
     signature_cards and tells, subtract what's already been seen (board +
     graveyard + revealed), and bucket by category. Probability scales with
     archetype confidence × remaining copies × turn (more copies still buried =
     higher probability of being in hand).
     - _target_priorities(opponent_board, opponent_archetype_record, guide, 
     role) → list[TargetPriority]. Cross-ref opponent's permanents against
     archetype.signature_cards (high priority) and guide.common_threats
     (matchup-specific priority). Role modulation: when AI is control (or
     flipped to control), target the opponent's clock; when AI is beatdown,
     target opponent's interaction/blockers.
