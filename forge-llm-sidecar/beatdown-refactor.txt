  How the Flores doctrine computes the role

  From advice.py:assess_role (lines 603–789), there are really three separate
  outputs, and they don't agree with each other:

  1. The role itself (ai_role) — starts from the deck's natural role and only
  flips under narrow conditions (lines 719–735):

  ai_role = ai_natural                       # from _natural_role(strategy_type)
  if ai_natural == "beatdown" and board_score < 0 and cards_score < 0:
      ai_role = "control"          # aggro deck behind on BOTH board AND cards
  elif ai_natural == "control" and board_score > 0.25 and cards_score >= 0 and
  human_life <= 12:
      ai_role = "beatdown"         # control deck only flips if life <= 12 too
  elif ai_natural == "contested":
      if board_score >= 0.25 or cards_score >= 0.25: ai_role = "beatdown"
      elif board_score <= -0.25 or cards_score <= -0.25: ai_role = "control"

  2. winning_side — a weighted blend of five axes, where board is only 30%
  (lines 738–750):

  score = 0.30*board + 0.25*cards + 0.25*clock + 0.10*tempo + 0.10*life

  3. board_score itself (lines 658–665) — power-sum delta / 6 (or count delta /
  4 if no P/T), clamped to [-1, 1].

  Why it gets the beatdown wrong

  The core problem: ai_role is anchored to the deck's natural role, not the 
  board. The board only matters at the margins, and the thresholds are
  asymmetric and harsh:

  - A control deck stays "control" even when crushing the board. To flip to
  beatdown it needs board_score > 0.25 AND cards_score >= 0 AND human_life <= 12
   (line 724). So an AI control deck that's way ahead on board but the human is
  still at 15 life → still labeled control, i.e. not the beatdown. Your rule
  says it should be the beatdown.
  - An aggro deck stays "beatdown" even when losing the board, unless it's
  behind on board AND cards simultaneously (line 721). Behind on board but even
  on cards → still "beatdown" while getting run over.
  - board_score >= 0.25 threshold means a real-but-modest board lead (say +0.15)
   registers as neither winning nor flipping — the dead zone keeps the natural
  role.
  - The dashboard/Forge can show conflicting signals: board_score positive (AI
  ahead on board) while ai_role says "control" and winning_side says "even"
  because the 30%-weighted blend got dragged down by clock/cards/tempo. That
  contradiction is probably what looked "so wrong" in the game.

  So "why was it so wrong": the doctrine is doing something more elaborate than
  "who's ahead on board" — it's preserving deck identity and requiring
  multi-axis agreement to flip. Whenever the board diverges from
  cards/clock/life, or sits in the ±0.25 dead zone, the role label stops
  tracking the board.
